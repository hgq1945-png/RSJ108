How To Be Mindful

Assembly time: 5 minutes
Awesome rating: 100% grounding

Feeling stressed, anxious, or out-of-whack? A mindfulness break can help reset your focus and bring you back into the present moment. No need for special equipment, just a few minutes to take a mental pause. Follow these simple steps to create a calming and simple ritual to create peace whenever life gets loud.

  • Things You'll Need

    • <
  • A quiet or low-distraction area
  • A comfortable seat or cushion
  • Headphones (optional, for audio)
  • A timer or phone (on Do Not Disturb)
  • Binder paper or notes app for reflections
  • An open-minded mindset
  1. Step 1: Choose and prepare your space.

    Find a spot where you can sit with minimal interruptions. It can be by a window, at your desk, or outside. Turn on Do Not Disturb or silence notifications. Sit upright but relaxed, with your feet grounded or legs crossed.

  2. Step 2: Set an intention for your break.

    Before you begin, decide your goal. Maybe you want to feel calmer, more focused, or simply present. Silently say your intention: “For the next five minutes, I’m giving myself permission to just be...”

    A calm space supports a calm mind.

  3. Step 3: Center on your breath with a guided practice.

    Close your eyes or soften your gaze. Notice your inhale and exhale without trying to control them. If you’d like support, follow this short guided mindfulness video to stay present during your break.

  4. Step 4: Expand awareness to body and sensations.

    Gently feel your energy from the top of your head down to your toes. Notice areas of tension, comfort, or numbness without titling them as good or bad. If your mind wanders, acknowledge it, let it be and return to your body.
